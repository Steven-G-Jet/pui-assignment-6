My critique

For Maps, its modular and minimalistic design make it very easy to use. The ease of use is especially important because the app is an all facing app for users across age and ability spectrum. In the context of my writing, my definition of module is that it is a information holding vessel. Within maps, there are two main types of modules: card and navigation.Both of them are shown below. Each module is designed to support a minimum number of interactions. Take an example, the card module only has four states of expansion, over the fold state, half-expanded state, full expansion state and full expansion state with the keyboard. Its limited states help users remember how to use them and navigate forward and back within the app. For the navigation, module, the only interactions one needs is swipe and tap. A feature I really love about the navigation is the zoom-in function . For a multi-step trip, the apple Maps helps users to focus on their current step by zooming in on it automatically.
